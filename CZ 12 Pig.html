Pig (亥猪)

1995, 2007, 2019, 2031, 2043

Introduction

Pig is the 12th animal in the 12-year cycle of the Chinese zodiac, coming after the Dog and before the Rat (which begins the cycle anew). Recent years of the Pig include 2019, 2007, 1995, 1983, 1971, 1959, and 1947, with the next Pig year in 2031 (Year of the Metal Pig). They are associated with the earthly branch sign hai.


In Chinese astrology, Pig is cool-headed and capable in any situation, detailed-oriented, intelligent, free-spirited, and a little too generous. They're passionate and enthusiastic, well-liked by friends who'll be there for Pig unconditionally because Pig was there for them.

How did Pig become a Chinese zodiac sign?

When talking about the pig, the first impressions that come to our mind might be obesity, laziness, or even stupidity. So, how did such an animal become one of the twelve Chinese zodiac signs? In traditional Chinese culture, the pig actually symbolizes wealth and good fortune. In ancient times, the pig was one of the important assets of families. Raising a fat pig was a sign of a family's prosperity and stable life. Moreover, the pig had a gentle nature and is considered to represent honesty and kindness. It dedicates its entire life to human, being the main source of meat for people. Given its significant role in human society, it's not hard to see why the pig was chosen as one of the twelve zodiac signs.


There is also an interesting legend about how the pig became one of the zodiac signs. On the day when the God of Heaven Jade Emperor selected the zodiac animals, it was stipulated that they must arrive at the Heavenly Palace within a certain time period, and only the first arrived twelve animals could become the zodiac signs. The pig knew itself slow, so it got up in the middle of the night and went towards the Heavenly Palace. Due to the long distance and numerous obstacles, the pig exerted all its strength and finally reached the Heavenly Palace. However, by then, the time limit set by the Jade Emperor had passed. Nevertheless, seeing how hard the pig had tried, the other animals pleaded for it. Eventually, the Jade Emperor was moved and allowed the pig to enter the Heavenly Palace, making it the last zodiac sign.


The twelfth position of the pig is closely related to the traditional Chinese timekeeping method. Among the twelve time slots, the twelfth one, “Hai”, represents 21:00 to 23:00. During this period, people always hear the sound of the pig nosing in the trough for food. To ensure the pig grows fat and strong, the owner has to get up and feed it. Therefore, “Hai” is naturally associated with the pig, and thus in the Chinese zodiac, the pig was also given the twelfth place.
