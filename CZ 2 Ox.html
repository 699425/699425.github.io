Ox (丑牛)

1985, 1997, 2009, 2021, 2033

Introduction

Ox is the second animal in the 12-year cycle of the Chinese zodiac, coming after the Rat and before the Tiger. Recent years of the Ox include 2021, 2009, 1997, 1985, 1973, 1961, and 1949, with the next Ox year in 2033 (Year of the Water Ox). The Year of the Ox corresponds with the earthly branch symbol chǒu.


Taciturn, upright, patient, unpretentious, and persevering, Ox is one of the least flashy yet one of the most likable of the zodiacs. Their practical mindset is both their greatest asset and, at times, their detriment.

How did Ox become a Chinese zodiac sign?

In ancient legends, the ox was the loyal servant of the God of Heaven Jade Emperor, constantly traveling between the celestial palace and the mortal world. One day, people sought help from the Jade Emperor, saying that the land in the mortal world was barren and requested the Jade Emperor to bestow grass seeds to restore vitality. The ox volunteered to go to the mortal world to sow grass seeds, but due to carelessness, it misunderstood the emperor's instructions of “a handful of seeds every three steps” as “three handfuls of seeds every one step” and caused wild grass to grow wantonly, even affecting the crops growing. Therefore, the Jade Emperor punished the ox to go to the mortal world and ordered it and its descendants to only eat grass and help people with farming. The ox knew its mistake and was willing to endure hardships and work diligently. It pulled carts and plowed fields without loosening the harness, doing a lot of work for people and earning people's praise. When selecting the zodiac sign, people unanimously elected it.


Why was such a diligent ox ranked second behind the tiny rat? Another legend has it that the Jade Emperor wanted to select twelve animals to be 12 zodiac signs, inviting all animals to participate in the selection. On the appointed day, the first twelve to arrive at the Jade Emperor's hall would be chosen as zodiac animals. The appointed day came, and the old ox set off early, crossing mountains and rivers, arriving at the Jade Emperor's hall. It thought it would be the first, but at this time, a rat jumped off its back and ran ahead of it, arriving at the Jade Emperor's hall first. It is said that the rat hid in the old ox's ears. When almost reaching the Jade Emperor's hall, it rushed out and got the first place. The old ox then became the second.
