The Zodiac

What is the Zodiac?

The zodiacs are divided into 12 equal parts, called “signs” that form a duration. There are two different set of signs that are called the zodiac; the Western astrology and the Eastern zodiac.

The Eastern (Chinese) and Western zodiac systems differ significantly in structure and focus—using years vs. months, and animals vs. constellations—but share deep similarities in their goal of interpreting personality, predicting future trends, and offering a, framework for understanding self and others. Both systems use a 12-part cycle to categorize human behavior.

Similarities

12-Fold Structure Both systems utilize 12 distinct signs to categorize personality and behavioral traits
Symbolic Archetypes Both use animals or specific, mythical, and elemental figures to represent human characteristics (e.g., the courage of a Leo vs. the diligence of an Ox)
Belief Systems Both function as frameworks to interpret life, fortune, and compatibility, providing a "mirror" for self-reflection
Cosmic Connection Both systems are rooted in observing the heavens to find meaning, using the movement of stars, planets, or, in the case of China, Jupiter's 12-year orbit

Difference: Eastern (Chinese) vs. Western

Time Calculation Western signs are based on the month of birth (position of the sun), while Chinese signs are based on the year of birth (lunar calendar)
Focus Western astrology focuses more on psychology, individuality, and personal development

Chinese astrology focuses more on destiny, social roles, and harmony
Elements Western astrology uses four elements (Fire, Earth, Air, Water)

Chinese astrology uses five elements (Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water)
Cycle The Chinese system operates on a 60-year cycle (combining 12 animals with 5 elements)
Origin Western astrology originates from Babylonian/Hellenistic traditions

Chinese astrology is rooted in Taoist philosophy and folklore

Western Zodiac

Western astrology is based on the position of the sun, moon and planets at the time of a person's birth. It is divided into 12 signs, each representing a different personality trait and set of characteristics.

Eastern Zodiac

Eastern zodiac, also known as Chinese zodiac, is based on a 12-year cycle, with each year associated with a specific animal sign. The 12 animal signs are Rat, Ox, Tiger, Rabbit, Dragon, Snake, Horse, Goat, Monkey, Rooster, Dog and Pig.
