Rabbit (卯兔)

1987, 1999, 2011, 2023, 2035

Introduction

Rabbit is the 4th animal in the 12-year cycle of the Chinese zodiac signs, coming after the Tiger and before the Dragon. Recent years of the Rabbit include 2023, 2011, 1999, 1987, 1975, 1963, and 1951, with the next Rabbit year in 2035 (Year of the Wood Rabbit). Rabbits are associated with the earthly branch symbol mǎo.


The Rabbit is open and optimistic, sensitive and imaginative, and enjoys the comfortable side of life. They're not the bravest of the zodiacs and may become neurotic if they choose to dwell on their emotions.

How did Rabbit become a Chinese zodiac sign?

Rabbits are often regarded as the embodiment of docility, agility and liveliness by Chinese people. Their fluffy appearance and lively behavior are deeply loved by people. In traditional Chinese culture, rabbits hold a unique and significant position. The jade rabbit in the Moon Palace, accompanying Goddess Chang'e, is busy making the elixir of immortality, adding a touch of warmth and vitality to the mysterious Moon Palace. Moreover, due to their strong reproductive ability, rabbits symbolize a large family with numerous offspring and prosperity. Given such special cultural significance, it's no wonder that rabbit became one of the zodiac signs.


Here is another explanation legendary. Rabbit and ox shared an amicable bond and often called each other brother. However, the rabbit had a penchant for showing off. Leveraging its fast running speed, it frequently mocked the ox. This spurred the determined ox to practice assiduously, and eventually, it became robust.


When the crucial moment for the ranking of the Twelve Chinese Zodiac Signs arrived, the rule was straightforward: the earlier an animal arrived, the higher its rank. The rabbit, elated upon hearing this, believed it had the upper hand. It made an agreement with the ox to set off together in the morning of that day. Yet, being naturally crafty, the rabbit didn't wait for the ox and set off alone much earlier. But this time, its cleverness proved counterproductive. With no other animals in sight and having started too early, the rabbit grew sleepy midway and decided to take a short nap.


While the rabbit dozed, the ox took the lead and ran towards the finish line, closely trailed by the tiger. Startled awake by the tiger, the rabbit hastily crossed the line, ultimately securing the fourth place, because the rat also arrived earlier hiding in the ox's ear. Even though the rabbit became a zodiac animal, it felt ashamed for losing to the ox it had previously mocked and moved its home to a ground cave, which is why rabbits live in caves nowadays.


In addition, the rabbit's fourth-place position in the zodiac is closely tied to the traditional Chinese time-keeping method. In the twelve time slots cycle, the fourth period of Mao from 05:00 to 07:00, when the sky starts to lighten, the rabbit is usually out foraging and most active. Thus, this period is associated with the rabbit. When ranking the twelve zodiac signs, the rabbit is accordingly placed fourth.
