The Zodiac

What is the Zodiac?

The zodiacs are divided into 12 equal parts, called “signs” that form a duration. There are two different set of signs that are called the zodiac; the Western astrology and the Eastern zodiac.


The Eastern (Chinese) and Western zodiac systems differ significantly in structure and focus—using years vs. months, and animals vs. constellations—but share deep similarities in their goal of interpreting personality, predicting future trends, and offering a, framework for understanding self and others. Both systems use a 12-part cycle to categorize human behavior.

Western Zodiac

Western astrology is based on the position of the sun, moon and planets at the time of a person's birth. It is divided into 12 signs, each representing a different personality trait and set of characteristics. The 12 constellation signs are Aries, Taurus, Cancer, Leo, Virgo, Libra, Scorpio, Sagittarius, Capricorn, Aquarius and Pisces.

Eastern Zodiac

Eastern zodiac, also known as Chinese zodiac or Sheng Xiao (生肖), is a repeating 12-year cycle of animal signs nd their ascribed attributes, based on the lunar calendar. The Lunar New Year or Spring Festival marks the transition from one animal to the next. It may be tricky to determine your Chinese zodiac sign if you are born in January or February, since Chinese New Year changes every year. The 12 animal signs are Rat, Ox, Tiger, Rabbit, Dragon, Snake, Horse, Goat, Monkey, Rooster, Dog and Pig.
