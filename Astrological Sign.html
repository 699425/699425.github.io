The Astrological Signs

What is it?

Astrological signs are twelve 30-degree sectors of the zodiac (Aries to Pisces) that correspond to the sun's position at birth, representing specific personality traits, elements (Fire, Earth, Air, Water), and modalities. Based on the Babylonian zodiac, these signs determine horoscopes, with key dates generally spanning from March 21 (Aries) to March 20 (Pisces).

History

Created by the Babylonians around 500 BCE, it was named "circle of animals" (zōdiakos kyklos) by the Greeks because most signs represent animals.

The Western zodiac system was developed by the Greeks and later refined by the Romans. It is based on the division of the ecliptic (the path of the sun across the sky) into 12 equal segments, each associated with a specific sign.

Why it matters

Astrological signs are often used in horoscopes to provide insights into a person's personality, relationships, and future. Many people find value in astrology as a tool for self-reflection and understanding others, although it is not scientifically supported.

How it works

Astrological signs are determined by the position of the sun at the time of a person's birth. Each sign is associated with specific personality traits, strengths, weaknesses, and compatibility with other signs. The zodiac is divided into four elements (Fire, Earth, Air, Water) and three modalities (Cardinal, Fixed, Mutable), which further influence the characteristics of each sign.

Four Elements:

  • Fire (Aries, Leo, Sagittarius): passionate, creative, confident, courageous
  • Earth (Taurus, Virgo, Capricorn): grounded, practical, stable, down-to-earth
  • Air (Gemini, Libra, Aquarius): intellectual, independent, communicative, social
  • Water (Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces): sensitive, intuitive, imaginative, nurturing

Qualities (also known as modalities):

  • Cardinal (Aries, Cancer, Libra, Capricorn): likes to initiate change
  • Fixed (Taurus, Leo, Scorpio, Aquarius): likes to sustain and maintain
  • Mutable (Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius, Pisces): likes to transform
