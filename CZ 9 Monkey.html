Monkey (申猴)

1992, 2004, 2016, 2028, 2040

Introduction

Monkey is the 9th animal in the 12-year cycle of the Chinese zodiac signs, coming after the Goat and before the Rooster. Recent years of the Monkey include 2016, 2004, 1992, 1980, 1968, 1956, and 1944, with the next Monkey year in 2028 (Year of the Earth Monkey). It is associated with the earthly branch sign shen.


In Chinese astrology, Monkey is intelligent, especially in analyzing and solving a problem. They excel at abstract thinking, as well as adapting to new people, tasks, and even environments. These master shapeshifters can fit in any time, anywhere.

How did Monkey become a Chinese zodiac sign?

There is an engaging legend explaining the monkey's inclusion in the twelve Chinese zodiac signs. Long ago, the tiger, renowned for subduing all the animals in the mountains, became the king of beasts. At the sight of the tiger, all other animals would scurry away in fear. Despite its proud status, the tiger often felt lonely. At that time, the monkey and the tiger were neighbors and had a close relationship. As time flowed by, the monkey gradually became the tiger's most capable and trusted assistant. On those occasions when the tiger had to be away from the forest, the monkey would assume the reins of forest management with great responsibility.


One day, the tiger unfortunately was caught by a net set by a hunter. It struggled desperately but couldn't free itself. Just then, the monkey happened to pass by. Hearing the tiger's loud cries for help, the monkey quickly climbed up the tree, untied the hunter's net rope, and rescued the tiger. After being saved, the tiger was extremely grateful to the monkey and promised to repay its kindness wholeheartedly in the future. From that moment on, their bond grew even stronger.


Many years later, when the God of Heaven Jade Emperor was selecting the zodiac animals, the tiger, as the king of beasts, was naturally chosen. The monkey also desired to become a zodiac animal. However, the Jade Emperor had a principle: only those who had done good deeds for humans were eligible. Since the monkey hadn't done any such deeds for humans, it wasn't initially selected. Then, the monkey begged the tiger to intercede with the Jade Emperor on its behalf. Remembering the monkey's life-saving act, the tiger did its utmost to plead with the Jade Emperor, praising the monkey's intelligence, resourcefulness, and the great help it had provided when the tiger was managing the forest. Eventually, the Jade Emperor agreed to include the monkey in the zodiac.


Why is the monkey ranked ninth in the Chinese zodiac?
The most prevalent explanation is closely tied to the traditional Chinese time-keeping method. In the ninth of the twelve time slots, known as “Shen” period, which spans from 15:00 to 17:00, monkeys are at their most vocal. During this period, their calls are the longest-lasting and loudest and they call more frequently than other time of the day. So, this time period was symbolized by the monkey. When it came to rank the twelve zodiac animals, the monkey was also placed in the ninth position.
