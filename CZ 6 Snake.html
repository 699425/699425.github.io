Snake (巳蛇)

1989, 2001, 2013, 2025, 2037

Introduction

Snake is the 6th animal in the 12-year cycle of the Chinese zodiac signs, coming after the Dragon and before the Horse. Recent years of the Snake include 2013, 2001, 1989, 1977, 1965, 1953, and 1941, with the next Snake year in 2037 (Year of the Fire Snake). Snake is associated with the earthly branch symbol si.


Snake is a cautious, introverted observer, cool and mysterious in behavior owing to their suspicious and insecure nature, yet their armor conceals abundant sensitivity, wisdom, and passion that will surely surprise their few confidants.

How did Snake become a Chinese zodiac sign?

In most cases, snake is considered evil, malevolence, and cattiness, and the elongated legless body always scares people. However, in ancient Chinese traditions, the snake once presented a venerated image and it is one of the earliest totems of Chinese nations. Chinese mother goddess Nüwa who said to have created humanity has the body of a snake and the head of a human. The skin-shedding of snake symbolizes “rebirth” and “renewal”. The Chinese dragon also has a snake body. Today, in some places in China, people still believe that a snake found in their courtyard can bring good luck. With such a high position in Chinese culture, it's not strange that the snake could be chosen as a zodiac sign.


There are also many interesting legends telling how. Below is one of the many:
Long long ago, there was a severe drought on the earth. The rivers and lakes were dry, the crops withered, and people had to go far away to fetch daily water. Knowing this, the God of Heaven Jade Emperor decided to send someone to help human beings on his behalf. He chose the clever and kind-hearted snake.


Coming down to the earth, the snake did not simply call for rain to save people from the drought; instead, he taught them to dig well to get underground water, build reservoirs to store rain, and cherish water in daily life, so they could survive even if there was another drought. Its good deeds not only touched human beings, but also impressed the Jade Emperor a lot and earned itself high praises. When selecting the zodiac signs, the Jade Emperor picked the snake.


Then why was snake ranked the sixth among all zodiac animals?
One of the most popular explanations goes like this, which is somehow related to the traditional Chinese time-calculator method: During the 6th time slot of 12, which is 09:00-11:00, snake is the most active, so used to represent that time period. When ranking the 12 zodiac animals, snake was put into the 6th position, too.
