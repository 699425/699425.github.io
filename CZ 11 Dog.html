Dog (戌狗)

1994, 2006, 2018, 2030, 2042

Introduction

Dog is the 11th animal in the 12-year cycle of the Chinese zodiac signs, coming after the Rooster and before the Pig. Recent years of the Dog include 2018, 2006, 1994, 1982, 1970, 1958, and 1946, with the next Dog year in 2030 (Year of the Metal Dog). It is associated with the earthly branch sign xu.


In Chinese astrology, Dog has a keen sense of justice, valor, and loyalty—betrayal is not part of their dictionary. They're courageous, helpful, humble, and kind-hearted, with a spirit of selfless dedication and an inviolable conduct of morality.

How did Dog become a Chinese zodiac sign?

Legend has it that when the God of Heaven Jade Emperor was choosing the twelve zodiac animals, he set a rule: any animal that was helpful to humans could be included in the zodiac. So each animal was eager to show its strengths to the Jade Emperor to prove that it was a reliable helper for humans.


Both cat and dog were close companions of human beings, but they didn't get along well. The cat thought the dog ate a lot but just lounged in front of the door. While the dog believed that the cat could do nothing but scare rat and made no contribution to human beings. They argued endlessly and at last came to the Heavenly Palace to ask the Jade Emperor to make a judgment.


The Jade Emperor asked them, “What do you do every day, and how much do you eat per meal?” The dog answered honestly, “I guard humans' houses every day and eat a basin of food per meal.” The cat thought for a while and replied, “I catch rat and only eat a small bowl of food per meal.” The cat cleverly told the Jade Emperor that it could help humans solve the rat problem and eat less. After hearing what the cat and the dog said, the Jade Emperor thought the cat ate less but did more, making greater contributions than the dog. The dog was very angry when it heard the result and considered that the cat won by lying, so it chased and bit the cat while cursing. The cat knew it was in the wrong and kept running without stopping. Even when it went home, it hid and didn't dare to come out.


However, when it actually was selecting the twelve zodiac animals, the officials in charge didn't follow the Jade Emperor's original order. Instead, they changed the rule to “the first twelve animals to arrive would become the twelve zodiac signs”.


When the dog learned about it, it went to the Heavenly Palace with the rooster to queue up to be one of the zodiac animals. The rooster flew and ran quickly, and got ahead of the dog. The cat, which had been hiding for a long time and didn't see the dog, came out and found that the dog had gone ahead to queue up to be a zodiac animal. It hurriedly ran to the Heavenly Palace and queued up behind the pig. At this time, there were exactly twelve animals. But the little rat played a trick and suddenly appeared to be the first zodiac animal. So the cat was in the thirteenth position and lost the chance to be a zodiac animal.


As for why the dog was ranked eleventh among the Chinese zodiac signs, a commonly recognized explanation is closely related to the traditional Chinese timekeeping method. In the twelve time slots cycle, the eleventh period “Xu” from 19:00 to 21:00, is when night falls and all is quiet. During this period, dog is the most alert, guarding his master and home, always ready to deal with potential dangers. Therefore, this period is represented by the dog. When ranking the Chinese zodiac signs, the dog naturally takes the eleventh place.
