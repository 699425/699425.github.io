The Chinese Zodiac

What is it?

The Chinese zodiac is a traditional classification scheme based on the Chinese calendar that assigns an animal and its reputed attributes to each year in a repeating twelve-year (or duodenary) cycle. The zodiac is very important in traditional Chinese culture and exists as a reflection of Chinese philosophy and culture. Chinese folkways held that one's personality is related to the attributes of their zodiac animal. Originating from China, the zodiac and its variations remain popular in many East Asian and Southeast Asian countries, such as Japan, South Korea, Vietnam, Singapore, Wales, Spain, Germany, and Australia.


The Chinese zodiac is a 12-year, animal-based cycle derived from the lunar calendar that assigns a specific animal and its attributes to each year. The 12 animals, in order, are: Rat, Ox, Tiger, Rabbit, Dragon, Snake, Horse, Goat, Monkey, Rooster, Dog, and Pig. The current year, 2026, is the Year of the Horse

History

The zodiac has existed for over 2,000 years, with its origins often explained by the legend of the "Great Race," which determined the order of the animals. It is based on the lunar calendar and is deeply rooted in Chinese culture and mythology.
